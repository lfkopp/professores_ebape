Professor Ronaldo Parente recebeu seu doutorado pela Temple University na Filadélfia (EUA) em Estratégia e Negócios Internacionais (2003). Tem mestrado em Administração com concentração em Finanças pela University of Tampa (1993) nos Estados Unidos. Graduado em Engenharia Civil pela Universidade de Fortaleza (1986). Sua área de pesquisa inclui estratégia, gestão de empresas multinacionais de países emergentes, outsourcing, modularização estratégica de processos produtivos, cadeia de suprimento global, inovação e gestão do conhecimento. Suas publicações aparecem (ou foram aceitas para publicação) em vários periódicos internacionais como o Journal of International Business Studies, Journal of Management Studies, Journal of International Management, IEEE Transactions on Professional Communication, Journal of the Academy of Marketing Science, International Business Review, Business Horizons, World Financial Review, Global Strategy Journal. Revista de Administracao de Empresas, Journal of Technology Forecasting and Social Change, International Journal of Automotive Technology & Management, e Journal of Behavioral and Applied Management. Professor Parente atualmente faz parte do corpo editorial dos seguintes periódicos internacionais: Journal of International Business Studies, Thunderbird International Business Review, Global Strategy Journal, e Journal of International Management. É também membro ativo e eleito no conselho da European International Business Academy. No lado profissional, Professor Parente trabalhou como consultor várias instituições publicas e privadas no Brasil, nos Estados Unidos, e na Bulgária. Também foi funcionário do Banco do Nordeste durante 13 anos. (Texto informado pelo autor)

Tacit Knowledge Integration Capability and Relational Resources
Descrição: Based on the integrative capability perspective (Henderson, 1994) and the relational view (Dyer & Singh, 1998), we examine how tacit knowledge integration capabilities (TKIC), a special case of dynamic capability, influence business performance. Our research context is the Brazilian automobile industry in which firms are currently experimenting with modular production and increasing their interactions with suppliers. Using a sample of automotive suppliers, we investigate how relational resources, such as the buyer?s trust in its suppliers and the level of supplier involvement, affect the level of TKIC, which in turn is hypothesized to be a source of competitive advantage. In addition, we examine the moderating effects of various communication media and cultural distance on the TKIC-business performance relationship. Our findings confirm the importance of relational resources and TKIC on business performance. We also find that supplier involvement is positively related to TKIC, and buyer?s trust has a partial indirect effect on TKIC, with the relationship being mediated by supplier involvement. Further, it appears that two of the cultural distance dimensions (i.e., power distance and masculinity) are also important moderators on the TKIC-business performance relationship.
