Patrick Gottfried Behr

I am an Associate Professor of Finance at the Brazilian School of Public and Business Administration (EBAPE) at the Getulio Vargas Foundation (FGV) in Rio de Janeiro. I do empirical research in the areas of Banking, Corporate Finance, and Microfinance and taught courses in Bank Risk Management, Financial Decision Making, Financial Markets and Instruments, Financial Institutions Management, Financial Intermediation, Introductory Accounting, and Microfinance at Coppead Graduate School of Business, EBAPE, Goethe University Frankfurt, National University of Singapore, and the University of Lugano. I published two books and several articles in major finance and management journals such as the Review of Finance, Journal of Financial and Quantitative Analysis, Management Science, the Journal of Banking and Finance, the Journal of Financial Services Research, and the Journal of Small Business Management. I did referee work for American Economic Review, Journal of Banking and Finance, Journal of Financial Intermediation, Management Science, Review of Finance, Review of Financial Studies, The Economic History Review, and World Development (among others) and was member of the program committees of/reviewer for the annual meetings of the Brazilian national association for research in business administration (Anpad), the Brazilian (SBFin), European (EFA), German Finance Association, and FIRS. My research was presented by me and coauthors at more than 60 international conferences (including AFA, EFA, FIRS), seminars, workshops in Asia, Europe, South America, and the U.S. I did consulting work for Brazilian and international companies and institutions such as Accenture, BNDES, the European Fund for Southeast Europe (EFSE), the German Stock Exchange, ProCredit Holding AG, and the OECD. From May 2013 to January 2017 I was a member of the board of directors of an international finance corporation active in the area of credit risk analysis. Since July 2015 I am a director of the Brazilian Finance Association (2 + 2 year terms). I am also the co-founder and since April 2016 Co-Head of the Center for Banking and Finance Research (CBFR) in Rio. I frequently comment on banking, rating and capital market issues in Brazilian and international media. (Texto informado pelo autor)

Projetos de pesquisa
2012 - 2015
EFSE Development Impact Study 2012-2015
Descrição: The European Fund for Southeast Europe provides funding to financial institutions with the aim of providing sustainable and responsible funding to micro and small enterprises helping them to grow, generate additional income and to create employment, as well as to low-income families assisting them in the improvement of their housing conditions. It does so with a strong commitment to responsible finance. The goal of the impact study is to assess whether EFSE's activities achieve their goals. For this purpose, six research projects will be conducted over the time period 2013-2015. Two of these projects will be in Armenia, three in Montenegro and one in Serbia. The project partner that was awarded the research grant is the European Banking Center (EBC) at the University of Tilburg. The project team consists of four professors of the EBC and one professor from EBAPE/FGV, Patrick Behr, who was invited to be a member of the EBC for the purpose of conducting the study. Prof. Behr will be the project leader responsible for the three projects referring to Montenegro..
Situação: Concluído; Natureza: Pesquisa.

Integrantes: Patrick Gottfried Behr - Integrante / Thorsten Beck - Coordenador / Vasso Iannadou - Integrante / Steven Ongena - Integrante / Maria Fabiana Penas - Integrante.
Financiador(es): European Fund for Southeast Europe - Auxílio financeiro.
